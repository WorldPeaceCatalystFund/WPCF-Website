Anyway, after studying so much math and science, I figured out a way to measure this effect - believe me or not. I've shown the heart effect to be real in the first experiment, which (link below). The experiment answers the question does the heart have an ability to draw forth what we seek from the randomness. The experiment is like this: Think of an animal in your heart/mind (there is a connection...), for example rabbit. Type the symbol animal into the single person usage of the application Icebreakers. Then see if the animal you thought of comes up within x number of images. This is the first experiment so I set x to 1200.

The scientific community will say that it shouldn't come up because the random chance probability is less than 1%. The app works by searching the internet for random images with the symbolic keyword you chose. So for this experiment, it searches the internet for images with the hashtag animal. There are trillions of images of animals on the internet. There are maybe millions of pictures of rabbits on the internet, but the overal % is less than 1%. You can argue this point, the numbers from my experiment show that it won't really matter.

31/38 times I was able to bring back a picture of the animal I sought which disproves the accepted rationale of random chance in favor of a heart effect. I extend this effect to questions - it can answer questions you ask in symbolic form. I further extend this effect to questions asked between two people - to reveal what separates us and what bonds us. These last two extensions, I have experiments designed but have not had the chance to run. In any event, if you have ever used your heart for anything, you should be able to see what I am saying.

So that's the heart effect. People can ask multiple other users the same question. It's rinse, wash, repeat with each conversation a heart based connect the dots game.
